Plant Hormones (Phytohormones)

1. What are Plant Hormones?

Plant hormones are chemical substances produced in small amounts in one part of the plant and transported to another part where they cause a response.

2. Types of Plant Hormones

  • Auxins
  • Gibberellins
  • Cytokinins
  • Abscisic Acid (ABA)
  • Ethylene

3. Auxins

Function:

  • Promote cell elongation
  • Responsible for phototropism and geotropism

Phototropism (Growth towards light)

Process (CAPS-style):

  1. Light falls on one side of the plant
  2. Auxins move to the shaded side
  3. Cells on the shaded side elongate more
  4. The plant bends towards the light

Geotropism (Response to gravity)

  • Roots show positive geotropism (grow downward)
  • Shoots show negative geotropism (grow upward)

4. Gibberellins

Function:

  • Stimulate stem elongation
  • Promote seed germination
  • Break dormancy in seeds and buds

Seed Germination Process:

  1. Water is absorbed by the seed
  2. Gibberellins are activated
  3. Enzymes are stimulated
  4. Food reserves are broken down into glucose
  5. Energy is released for growth
  6. The embryo grows into a seedling

5. Cytokinins

Function:

  • Promote cell division (mitosis)
  • Promote growth of lateral buds
  • Delay ageing (senescence)

Effect on Apical Dominance:

  • Auxins from the apical bud inhibit lateral buds
  • Cytokinins promote lateral growth
  • Removing the apical bud allows lateral buds to grow

6. Abscisic Acid (ABA)

Function:

  • Inhibits growth
  • Promotes dormancy in seeds
  • Helps plants survive stressful conditions

Role in Stomatal Closure:

  1. During water stress, ABA is produced
  2. ABA causes stomata to close
  3. This reduces water loss through transpiration

7. Ethylene

Function:

  • Stimulates fruit ripening
  • Promotes leaf and fruit drop (abscission)

Fruit Ripening Process:

  1. Ethylene is produced by the fruit
  2. Breakdown of chlorophyll occurs
  3. Starch is converted into sugars
  4. Fruit softens and changes colour

8. Summary Table

Hormone Main Function
Auxins Cell elongation, phototropism
Gibberellins Stem elongation, seed germination
Cytokinins Cell division, delay ageing
Abscisic Acid Inhibits growth, stomatal closure
Ethylene Fruit ripening, leaf drop

9. Exam Tips (CAPS Style)

  • Always write processes in correct order
  • Use correct terminology (e.g. elongation, dormancy, abscission)
  • Explain movement of hormones clearly
  • Link hormone function to plant response

10. Summary

  • Plant hormones control growth and development
  • They are transported from one part of the plant to another
  • Each hormone has a specific function
