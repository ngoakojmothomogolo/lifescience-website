Human Reproduction

1. Introduction

Reproduction is the biological process by which new individuals are produced from parents.

Humans reproduce sexually, involving the fusion of male and female gametes.

2. Male Reproductive System

  • Testes: Produce sperm and testosterone
  • Epididymis: Stores and matures sperm
  • Vas deferens: Transports sperm
  • Urethra: Passage for sperm and urine
  • Penis: Organ of copulation

3. Female Reproductive System

  • Ovaries: Produce ova and hormones
  • Fallopian tubes: Site of fertilisation
  • Uterus: Site of development of embryo
  • Endometrium: Uterine lining
  • Vagina: Birth canal

4. Gametogenesis

4.1 Spermatogenesis

Process:

  1. Occurs in the testes
  2. Diploid cells in the seminiferous tubules of the testes,
  3. undergo meiosis
  4. to form haploid sperm cells.

4.2 Oogenesis

Process:

  1. Occurs in the ovaries
  2. Diploid cells in the ovary undergo mitosis
  3. to form numerous follicles.
  4. At the onset of puberty
  5. and under the influence of FSH,
  6. one cell inside a follicle enlarges and undergoes meiosis.
  7. Of the four cells that are produced, only one survives to form a mature, haploid ovum.
  8. This occurs in a monthly cycle.

5. Structure of Sperm Cell

  • Head: Contains nucleus with genetic material
  • Acrosome: Contains enzymes to penetrate ovum
  • Midpiece: Contains mitochondria for energy
  • Tail: Enables movement

6. Structure of Ovum

  • Large and spherical
  • Contains nutrients
  • Surrounded by protective layers
  • Non-motile

7. Menstrual Cycle (VERY IMPORTANT)

The menstrual cycle is a monthly cycle that prepares the uterus for pregnancy.

Phases:

7.1 Menstruation

  • Uterine lining is shed
  • Blood is expelled

7.2 Follicular Phase

  • FSH stimulates development of follicles
  • Oestrogen is secreted
  • Endometrium thickens

7.3 Ovulation

The release of an ovum from the ovary.

Process:

  1. LH surge occurs
  2. Follicle ruptures
  3. Ovum is released into the fallopian tube

7.4 Luteal Phase

  • Corpus luteum forms
  • Progesterone is secreted
  • Endometrium is maintained

8. Fertilisation

Process:

  1. Sperm is deposited in the vagina
  2. Sperm swim through the cervix and uterus
  3. Sperm reaches the fallopian tube
  4. One sperm penetrates the ovum
  5. Nuclei of sperm and ovum fuse
  6. A zygote is formed

9. Implantation

Process:

  1. Zygote divides by mitosis to form a blastocyst
  2. Blastocyst reaches the uterus
  3. Implants into the endometrium

10. Development of the Embryo

  • Cells differentiate into tissues and organs
  • Placenta develops
  • Umbilical cord connects embryo to placenta

11. Role of the Placenta

  • Allows exchange of nutrients and gases
  • Removes waste products
  • Prevents mixing of maternal and fetal blood
  • Produces hormones (oestrogen and progesterone)

12. Birth (Labour)

Process:

  1. Hormonal changes trigger contractions
  2. Cervix dilates
  3. Uterine contractions push the baby out
  4. Baby is delivered through the vagina

13. Summary (Exam Ready)

  • Fertilisation occurs in the fallopian tube
  • Menstrual cycle is controlled by hormones (FSH, LH, oestrogen, progesterone)
  • Implantation occurs in the uterus
  • Placenta supports the developing foetus
