Responding to the Environment (Humans)

1. Introduction

Humans respond to stimuli using the nervous system and endocrine system.

A stimulus is any change in the internal or external environment.

2. Structure of a Neuron

  • Dendrites: Receive impulses
  • Cell body: Contains nucleus
  • Axon: Conducts impulses away from cell body
  • Myelin sheath: Insulates axon and speeds up impulses
  • Axon terminals: Transmit impulses to next neuron

3. Types of Neurons

  • Sensory neuron: Carries impulses from receptors to CNS
  • Motor neuron: Carries impulses from CNS to effectors
  • Interneuron: Connects sensory and motor neurons

4. Reflex Arc (VERY IMPORTANT)

A reflex action is a rapid, automatic response of the body to a stimulus.

Process:

  1. Stimulus is detected by receptor
  2. Sensory neuron carries impulse to spinal cord
  3. Impulse is passed to interneuron
  4. Interneuron passes impulse to motor neuron
  5. Motor neuron carries impulse to effector
  6. Effector responds (e.g. muscle contracts)

5. Synapse

A synapse is the junction between two neurons.

Process of Transmission:

  1. Impulse reaches axon terminal
  2. Neurotransmitters are released
  3. Neurotransmitters cross synaptic gap
  4. They bind to receptors on next neuron
  5. New impulse is generated

6. Central Nervous System (CNS)

  • Brain: Controls body activities
  • Spinal cord: Controls reflex actions

7. Structure and Functions of the Brain

  • Cerebrum: Thinking, memory, voluntary actions
  • Cerebellum: Balance and coordination
  • Medulla oblongata: Controls involuntary actions (breathing, heartbeat)

8. The Eye (VERY IMPORTANT)

Functions of parts:

  • Cornea: Refracts light
  • Iris: Controls size of pupil
  • Pupil: Allows light into eye
  • Lens: Focuses light onto retina
  • Retina: Contains photoreceptors
  • Optic nerve: Carries impulses to brain

9. Accommodation (VERY IMPORTANT PROCESS)

9.1 Viewing a Near Object

  1. Ciliary muscles contract
  2. Suspensory ligaments slacken/relax
  3. Lens becomes thicker (more convex)
  4. Light is refracted more
  5. Image is focused on retina

9.2 Viewing a Distant Object

  1. Ciliary muscles relax
  2. Suspensory ligaments tighten
  3. Lens becomes thinner (less convex)
  4. Light is refracted less
  5. Image is focused on retina

10. Pupillary Mechanism (VERY IMPORTANT)

10.1 Bright Light

  1. Light intensity increases
  2. Circular muscles contract
  3. Radial muscles relax
  4. Pupil constricts
  5. Less light enters eye

10.2 Dim Light

  1. Light intensity decreases
  2. Circular muscles relax
  3. Radial muscles contract
  4. Pupil dilates
  5. More light enters eye

11. Homeostasis (Link to Nervous System)

The nervous system helps maintain a stable internal environment by detecting changes and coordinating responses.

12. Summary (Exam Ready)

  • Stimulus → response is controlled by nervous system
  • Reflex arc is rapid and automatic
  • Neurons transmit impulses
  • Accommodation focuses images
  • Pupil controls light entry
