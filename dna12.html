DNA and RNA: The Code of Life

1. What is DNA?

DNA (Deoxyribonucleic Acid) is the genetic material found in all living organisms. It contains the instructions for building proteins and controlling cell activities.

Structure:

  • Double helix (twisted ladder shape)
  • Made of nucleotides
  • Each nucleotide consists of:
    • Phosphate group
    • Deoxyribose sugar
    • Nitrogenous base

2. Nitrogenous Bases

There are 4 nitrogenous bases in DNA:

  • Adenine (A)
  • Thymine (T)
  • Cytosine (C)
  • Guanine (G)

Base pairing rule:

  • A pairs with T
  • C pairs with G

3. Structure of DNA

  • Two strands run in opposite directions (antiparallel)
  • Bases are held together by hydrogen bonds

4. Functions of DNA

  • Stores genetic information
  • Controls protein synthesis
  • Responsible for inheritance of characteristics

5. RNA (Ribonucleic Acid)

RNA is a single-stranded molecule involved in protein synthesis.

Differences between DNA and RNA:


DNA RNA
Double-stranded Single-stranded
Contains deoxyribose sugar Contains ribose sugar
Has thymine Has uracil

6. Types of RNA

  • mRNA (messenger RNA) -carries genetic code from DNA to ribosome.
  • tRNA (transfer RNA) - brings amino acids to ribosome.
  • rRNA (ribosomal RNA)-forms part of the ribosome.

7. DNA Replication

DNA replication is the process where DNA makes an exact copy of itself.

Steps:

  1. DNA double helix unwinds
  2. Hydrogen bonds break
  3. Each strand acts as a template
  4. Free nucleotides pair according to base pairing rules
  5. Two identical DNA molecules are formed

Important: This process is semi-conservative (each new DNA contains one original strand and one new strand).

8. Importance of DNA

  • Ensures continuity of life
  • Allows inheritance of traits
  • Controls cell activities through protein synthesis

9. Quick Summary

  • DNA = double helix, stores genetic information
  • RNA = single strand, helps in protein synthesis
  • Base pairing: A-T, C-G
  • DNA replication is semi-conservative
