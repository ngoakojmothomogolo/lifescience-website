Responding to the Environment (Plants)

1. Introduction

Plants respond to stimuli in their environment using growth movements.

These responses are controlled by plant hormones (especially auxins).

2. Types of Plant Responses

  • Tropisms: Directional growth responses
  • Nastic movements: Non-directional responses

3. Tropisms (VERY IMPORTANT)

3.1 Phototropism

Growth response to light.

Example: Shoots grow towards light (positive phototropism)

Process (CAPS style):

  1. Light falls on one side of the shoot
  2. Auxins move to the shaded side
  3. Cells on the shaded side elongate more
  4. The shoot bends towards the light

3.2 Geotropism (Gravitropism)

Growth response to gravity.

  • Roots show positive geotropism (grow downward)
  • Shoots show negative geotropism (grow upward)

Process (Roots):

  1. Gravity is detected
  2. Auxins accumulate on the lower side of the root
  3. High auxin concentration inhibits growth
  4. Upper side grows faster
  5. Root bends downward

Process (Shoots):

  1. Gravity is detected
  2. Auxins accumulate on the lower side
  3. Cells on lower side elongate more
  4. Shoot bends upward

3.3 Thigmotropism

Growth response to touch.

Example: Tendrils wrapping around support

Process:

  1. Plant comes into contact with support
  2. Auxins redistribute
  3. Cells on one side grow faster
  4. Plant coils around support

4. Nastic Movements

Movements that are not dependent on the direction of the stimulus.

Example: Opening and closing of flowers

  • Caused by changes in turgor pressure

5. Role of Auxins (VERY IMPORTANT)

  • Produced in the tips of shoots
  • Cause cell elongation
  • Control tropic responses

Auxins move away from light and accumulate in shaded areas.

6. Apical Dominance

The apical bud inhibits the growth of lateral buds.

Explanation:

  • Auxins produced at the tip suppress lateral buds
  • If the tip is removed, lateral buds grow

7. Summary Table

Response Stimulus Example
Phototropism Light Shoot grows towards light
Geotropism Gravity Roots grow downward
Thigmotropism Touch Tendrils coil

8. Summary (Exam Ready)

  • Plants respond to stimuli using growth movements
  • Tropisms are directional responses
  • Auxins control plant responses
  • Phototropism and geotropism are commonly tested
