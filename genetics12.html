Genetics and Inheritance

1. What is Genetics?

Genetics is the study of heredity and how characteristics are passed from parents to offspring.

Inherited characteristics are determined by genes located on chromosomes.

2. Key Terms

  • Gene: A section of DNA that codes for a specific trait
  • Alleles: Different forms of the same gene
  • Dominant allele: Expressed when present (represented by capital letter)
  • Recessive allele: Only expressed when two copies are present (lowercase)
  • Genotype: Genetic makeup (e.g. TT, Tt, tt)
  • Phenotype: Physical appearance (e.g. tall, short)
  • Homozygous: Two identical alleles (TT or tt)
  • Heterozygous: Two different alleles (Tt)

3. Mendel’s Laws

3.1 Law of Dominance

When two different alleles are present, the dominant allele is expressed and the recessive allele is masked.

3.2 Law of Segregation

Alleles separate during gamete formation so that each gamete receives only one allele of each gene.

3.3 Law of Independent Assortment

Different gene pairs separate independently of one another during meiosis.

4. Monohybrid Crosses

A monohybrid cross involves one trait controlled by a single gene.

Example: Tall (T) is dominant over short (t)

Cross: Tt × Tt

T t
T TT Tt
t Tt tt

Genotypic ratio: 1 TT : 2 Tt : 1 tt

Phenotypic ratio: 3 Tall : 1 Short

5. Dihybrid Crosses

A dihybrid cross involves two traits.

Example: Seed shape (R = round, r = wrinkled) and seed colour (Y = yellow, y = green)

Cross: RrYy × RrYy

Phenotypic ratio: 9 : 3 : 3 : 1

  • 9 Round Yellow
  • 3 Round Green
  • 3 Wrinkled Yellow
  • 1 Wrinkled Green

6. Incomplete Dominance

Neither allele is dominant, resulting in a blend of traits.

Example: Red (RR) × White (WW) → Pink (RW)

7. Codominance

Both alleles are equally expressed in the phenotype.

Example: Blood group AB (IAIB)

8. Multiple Alleles

A gene can have more than two alleles in a population.

Example: Human blood groups (IA, IB, i)

  • IA and IB are codominant
  • i is recessive

9. Sex Determination

In humans:

  • Female: XX
  • Male: XY

The male determines the sex of the child.

10. Pedigree Diagrams

Pedigree diagrams show the inheritance of traits through generations.

  • Square = male
  • Circle = female
  • Shaded = affected

11. Summary

  • Genes control inherited traits
  • Alleles can be dominant or recessive
  • Monohybrid and dihybrid crosses determine ratios
  • Genetics explains variation in organisms
