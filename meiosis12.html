Meiosis

1. What is Meiosis?

Meiosis is a type of cell division that produces four haploid cells (gametes) from one diploid parent cell.

It is important for sexual reproduction because it reduces the chromosome number by half.

2. Importance of Meiosis

  • Produces gametes (sperm and ova)
  • Ensures chromosome number is halved (diploid → haploid)
  • Introduces genetic variation

3. Key Terms

  • Diploid (2n): Cells with two sets of chromosomes
  • Haploid (n): Cells with one set of chromosomes
  • Homologous chromosomes: Pair of chromosomes (one from each parent)
  • Gametes: Sex cells (sperm and ova)

4. Stages of Meiosis

Meiosis I (Reduction Division)

  • Prophase I: Chromosomes condense; homologous chromosomes pair up (synapsis); crossing over occurs
  • Metaphase I: Homologous chromosomes line up at the equator
  • Anaphase I: Homologous chromosomes separate
  • Telophase I: Two haploid cells are formed

Meiosis II (Similar to Mitosis)

  • Prophase II: Chromosomes condense again
  • Metaphase II: Chromosomes line up at the equator
  • Anaphase II: Sister chromatids separate
  • Telophase II: Four haploid cells are formed

5. Crossing Over

Crossing over occurs during Prophase I when non-sister chromatids of homologous chromosomes exchange genetic material.

This leads to genetic variation.

6. Independent Assortment

During Metaphase I, homologous chromosomes arrange randomly at the equator.

This results in different combinations of chromosomes in gametes, increasing variation.

7. Genetic Variation

  • Crossing over
  • Independent assortment
  • Random fertilisation

8. Differences: Meiosis vs Mitosis

Meiosis Mitosis
Produces 4 haploid cells Produces 2 diploid cells
Occurs in reproductive organs Occurs in somatic (body) cells
Genetically different cells Genetically identical cells
Includes crossing over No crossing over

9. Summary

  • Meiosis produces 4 haploid gametes
  • It reduces chromosome number (2n → n)
  • Creates genetic variation
  • Includes crossing over and independent assortment
