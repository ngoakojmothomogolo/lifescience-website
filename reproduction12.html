Reproduction in Vertebrates

1. Introduction

Reproduction in vertebrates can be either:

  • Asexual reproduction (not common in vertebrates)
  • Sexual reproduction (most common)

Sexual reproduction involves the fusion of male and female gametes.

2. Types of Fertilisation

2.1 External Fertilisation

Fertilisation occurs outside the body of the female, usually in water.

Examples: Fish and amphibians

Process:

  1. Female releases eggs into water
  2. Male releases sperm over the eggs
  3. Sperm swims to the egg
  4. Fertilisation occurs outside the body

Advantages:

  • Large number of offspring produced

Disadvantages:

  • Low survival rate
  • Gametes exposed to predators and environmental conditions

2.2 Internal Fertilisation

Fertilisation occurs inside the female body.

Examples: Reptiles, birds, mammals

Process:

  1. Sperm is transferred into the female reproductive tract
  2. Sperm travels to the egg
  3. Fertilisation occurs inside the female
  4. Zygote is formed

Advantages:

  • Higher chance of survival

Disadvantages:

  • Fewer offspring produced

3. Types of Development

3.1 Ovipary (Egg-laying)

Embryo develops outside the body in an egg.

  • Fertilisation may be internal or external
  • Embryo develops using yolk as a food source

Examples: Fish, amphibians, birds

3.2 Vivipary (Live birth)

Embryo develops inside the body and is born alive.

  • Fertilisation is internal
  • Embryo is nourished via the placenta

Examples: Mammals

3.3 Ovovivipary

Eggs develop inside the female body, but there is no placenta.

  • Embryo gets nourishment from yolk
  • Young are born alive

Examples: Some fish and reptiles

4. Amniotic Egg

An amniotic egg allows development on land.

Structures:

  • Amnion: Protects embryo in fluid
  • Yolk sac: Provides nutrients
  • Allantois: Stores waste
  • Chorion: Allows gas exchange

Important: Found in reptiles, birds, and some mammals.

5. Placenta (Mammals)

The placenta is an organ that allows exchange between mother and foetus.

Functions:

  • Supplies oxygen and nutrients
  • Removes waste
  • Produces hormones
  • Prevents mixing of blood

6. Parental Care

Parental care increases the chances of survival of offspring.

Examples:

  • Protecting young
  • Feeding young
  • Building nests

7. Comparison: External vs Internal Fertilisation

External Fertilisation Internal Fertilisation
Occurs outside body Occurs inside body
Requires water Does not require water
Low survival rate High survival rate
Many offspring Fewer offspring

8. Summary (Exam Ready)

  • Reproduction in vertebrates is mostly sexual
  • Fertilisation can be external or internal
  • Development can be ovipary, vivipary, or ovovivipary
  • Amniotic egg allows reproduction on land
  • Parental care increases survival
